GEOADS03T – Geotectonics and Geomorphology

Credits: 3 (45 Hours of Teaching)

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the Earth’s structural and tectonic evolution along with the fundamental processes shaping its surface. Emphasis is placed on integrating geotectonic principles with geomorphological processes to explain landscape development over geological time scales.

Unit I: Geotectonics

1. Earth’s Tectonic and Structural Evolution

  • Study of the Earth’s formation and structural evolution with reference to the Geological Time Scale.
  • Special emphasis on the Pleistocene epoch and its tectonic significance.

2. Isostasy

  • Concept of isostatic equilibrium and its role in crustal movements.
  • Models of Airy and Pratt, with discussion on their applicability.

3. Plate Tectonics: Unified Theory of Global Tectonics

  • Principles of plate tectonics as a unifying framework for understanding Earth’s tectonic behavior.
  • Processes and landforms associated with plate margins (divergent, convergent, transform) and hotspots.

4. Folds and Faults

  • Formation mechanisms of folds and faults.
  • Classification of folds (anticlines, synclines, monoclines) and faults (normal, reverse, thrust, strike-slip).

Unit II: Geomorphology

5. Degradational Processes

  • Weathering: Physical, chemical, and biological processes.
  • Mass wasting: Types, mechanisms, and controlling factors.
  • Resultant landforms from weathering and mass movements.

6. Development of River Networks and Associated Landforms

  • Evolution of drainage systems on uniclinal and folded structures.
  • Surface expression of faults on landscapes and rivers.

7. Coastal Processes and Landforms

  • Dynamics of waves, tides, and currents.
  • Formation and classification of coastal landforms (cliffs, beaches, estuaries).

8. Glacial and Glacio-Fluvial Processes and Landforms

  • Mechanisms of glacial erosion, transportation, and deposition.
  • Landforms created by glaciers and meltwater, including moraines, drumlins, and outwash plains.

9. Aeolian and Fluvio-Aeolian Processes and Landforms

  • Wind-driven processes and formation of deserts and dune landscapes.
  • Interaction of fluvial and aeolian processes in shaping landforms.

10. Models of Landscape Evolution

  • Comparative study of the Davisian, Penckian, and Hackian models.
  • Insights into long-term landscape development and geomorphic cycles.
